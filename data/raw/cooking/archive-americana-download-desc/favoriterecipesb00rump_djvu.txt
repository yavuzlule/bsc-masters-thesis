1  c.  softened  butter 
1  1/2  c.  sugar 

3  c.  flour  (all  purpose  plain) 
1/2  t.  soda 

1/4  t.  salt 

3/4  c.  buttermilk 

4  eggs. 

Combine  butterscotch  morsels,  coffee  and 
water  in  heavy  saucepan.  Cook  over  low 
heat,  stirring  until  melted  (do  not  boil) 
Cool  to  lukewarm.  Cream  butter,  add  sugar 
gradually,  creaming  until  light  and 
fluffy,  blend  in  butterscotch  mixture. 
Add  flour  alternately  with  buttermilk. 
Blend  at  low  speed.  Beat  2  minutes  at 
medium  speed,  scraping  down  bowl.  Add 
eggs,  one  at  a  time,  beating  1  minute 
after  each.  Pour  into  greased  and  floured 
tube  pan.  Bake  at  350  for  1  hour.

3  to  4  tablespoons  of  the  pineapple 
liquid  -  mix  well  -  spread  on  cookies 
while  warm.  This  makes  3  1/2  dozen. 
Recipe  may  be  doubled  or  tripled.

added  but  dough  should  be  very  stiff).

Dissolve  yeast  in  warm  water,  lukewarm  if 
you  plan  to  bake  the  rolls  immediately  - 
otherwise  dissolve  in  1/2  c.  warm  water  and  1 
%  c.  cold  water  for  placing  in  refrigerator.

and  add  yeast  water,  mix  with  a  wooden

bowl,  cover  and  let  dough  rise  30  minutes. 
If  baking  another  day,  cover  and  place  in 
refrigerator.  When  ready  to  bake  rolls,  pat 
out  gently  on  floured  board,  cut  and  place 
on  greased  baking  pan.  Cover  with  warm 
damp  cloth  and  let  rise  45  min.  Brush 
lightly  with  butter  and  bake  in  450  degree 
oven  for  15  minutes.
