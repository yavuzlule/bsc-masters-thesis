HOUSEHOLD  NOTES 


Taken  from  the  Best  by  Test  cook  book  which  was 
published  by  the  Sewing  Circle  of  Centenary 
Church  in  1907. 

A  tablespoon  of  soda  added  to  one  quart  of  water 
and  boiling  in  the  coffee  pot  for  an  hour  will 
thoroughly  cleanse  the  pot.     Rinse  well. 

Do  not  save  old  trash  thinking  it  may  be  used 
someday.     The  time  and  patience  caused  in  hand- 
ling it  cannot  be  compensated  for  by  it. 

A  little  milk  added  to  water  in  which  silver  is 
washed  will  help  to  keep  it  bright. 

A  little  soap  applied  with  point  of   lead  pencil 
will  remedy  a  squeaking  hinge. 

A  spot  on  polished  wood  made  by  placing  a  heated 
dish  on  it  will  disappear  if  a  little  salt  and 
salad  oil  is  poured  on  it  and  allowed  to  remain 
for  an  hour  or  so. 

Butter  will  remove  almost  any  kind  of  stain  if 
rubbed  well  into  it,   and  washed  out  immediately 
with  hot  water  and  pure  soap. 

TO  MAKE  GOOD  VINEGAR 

Put  four  gallons  of  warm  rain  water,   one  gallon 
of  molasses,   two  quarts  of  yeast  in  an  open 
cast,   spreading  a  piece  of  thin  muslin  over  the 
top.     Cover  the  cast  at  night  and  during  rain 
storms.     In  three  or  four  weeks  you  will  have 
excellent  vinegar.

Raw  Rice  As  A  Cleaner 


#7658' 


If  the  housewife  who  has  narrow  necked  vases, 
babies'  bottles,  fruit  jars,  etc.,  to  clean 
will  pour  some  water  and  raw  rice  into  them, 
and  give  a  vigorous  shaking,  she  will  be  sur- 
prised at  the  results .  Some  use  shot  for  the 
purpose,  but  rice  is  infinitely  better,  as  it 
polished  as  well  as  cleans.

SKILLET  BREAD 


Barbara  Terrell 


2/3  C.  self  rising  meal 
2  tsp.  baking  powder 
1/2  tsp.  salt 


1  C .  milk 
1  egg 

4  tbs .  melted  fat 


Put  one  half  fat  in  skillet,  place  in  pre-heated 
oven  to  heat  while  mixing  bread.     Mix  wet  in- 
gredients, mix  dry,   then  combine.     Pour  into  hot 
skillet,  bake  20  minutes  at  425°   ,   or  until  top 
is  crusty  brown.     This  recipe  doubles  and  tri- 
ples easily,  but  for  best  results  must  be  cooked 
in  an  iron  skillet.

HOT  ROLLS  Thelma  Scott 

1  pkg .  dry  yeast  2  tbs.  sugar 

1/4  C.  water  1  tsp.  salt 

1  C.  milk  /   scalded  1  well-beaten  egg 

2  tbs.   shortening  3  1/2  C.  sifted  flour 

Soften  yeast  in  1/4  C.   lukewarm  water.  Combine 
milk,   shortening,   sugar  and  salt;  cool  to  luke- 
warm,  add  softened  yeast,   egg.     Gradually  stir 
in  flour  to  form  soft  dough.     Beat  well,  cover 
and  let  rise  in  warm  place  until  double  in  bulk, 
about  2  hours,  knead  again.     Shape  as  desired, 
place  in  greased  pan,   cover  and  set  in  warm  place 
until  double  in  size.  Bake  at  400°  20  to  25  min.

MRS  .   THOMPSON  '  S  BREAD 


Florrie  Mercer 


2  C.  milk 

2  pkg .  dry  yeast 

2  1/2  tsp.  salt 

Flour 


5  tbs .  sugar 
4  heaping  tbs . 

shortening 


Scald  milk  and  let  stand  until  tepid  warm.  Put 
yeast  into  one  cup  lukewarm  water  which  has  one 
tbs.   sugar  dissolved  in  it.     To  the  cooled  milk 
add  yeast  and  enough  flour  to  make  a  very  light 
dough.     Let  rise  until  light (about  45  min .  or 
1  hour).     Beat  down  and  add  sugar,   salt,  short- 
ening,  and  enough  flour  to  make  a  very  stiff 
dough.  Turn  bread  onto  floured  board  at  this 
point  and  knead.     Shape  and  put  into  greased  bowl 
Let  rise  until  light (about  1  hour).     Stir  down 
and  turn  out  on  floured  dough  board.  Cut  dough 
off  into  desired  sizes,  dip  in  melted  butter, and 
put  in  pans.     Let  rise  until  double  in  size. Bake 
in  oven  400P   for  first  15  min.  and  then  let  tem- 
perature drop  to  375°  and  bake  until  done. 

DUTCH  BREAD  Florrie  Mercer 

The  above  recipe  makes  3  regular  loaves  of  bread. 
You  may  bake  two  loaves  and  use  the  third  por- 
tion for  dutch  bread  or  vice  versa. 

When  dough  is  ready  to  be  made  into  loaf,  just 
spread  it  out  on  board  gently.     Add  raisins  if 
desired.     Knead  to  distribute  raisins.  Spread 
dough  out  by  patting  and  pulling  until  about  1/2 
inch  thick.     Place  in  greased  pan  or  cookie 
sheet,  punch  with  fingers.     Pour  melted  butter 
over  bread (brush  with  pastry  brush),   then  put 
mixture  of  cinnamon  and  sugar  over  top.  Let 
rise  and  bake.     Bread  may  be  frozen  immediately 
after  it  has  cooled,   if  desired.     To  serve,  thaw, 
heat  in  oven  wrapped  in  foil.   Slice  as  desired.

ALABAMA  BUTTERMILK  LIGHT  ROLLS  Eva  Edwards 


2  tbs .  sugar 

4  tbs.  shortening 

1  pkg-  dry  yeast 
Warm  Water  for  yeast 

2  C.  buttermilk 


4  to  6  C.  flour 

1  tsp.  soda 

1  tsp.  baking  powder 

1  tsp.  salt 


Cream  sugar  and  shortening.     Dissolve  yeast  in 
cup  with  the  same  quantity  of  warm  water,  fill 
rest  of  cup  with  buttermilk,  and  set  aside. 
Sift  4  C.  flour  with  dry  ingredients.  Alternate 
adding  flour,  buttermilk,   and  yeast  mixture,  and 
the  second  cup  of  buttermilk  and  shortening.  Work 
in  enough  flour  to  make  a  soft  dough;  this  should 
take  between  4  and  6  cups.     Put  dough  in  bowl  and 
place  in  refrigerator.     The  unusual  feature  of  it 
is  that  the  dough  will  rise  quickly  once  it  has 
been  thoroughly  chilled.     If  desired,  the  rising 
can  be  forced  by  placing  the  made-out  dough  in 
around  80°  temperature.     Bake  at  400°   for  15  to 
2  0  minutes .

SPICED  MEAT  BALLS       \S  Eddie  Long 

Mix  8  lbs.  beef  with  2  lbs.  prepared  meat  loaf 
(pork,   veal,  beef).     Soak  a  small  loaf  of  pumper- 
nickle  bread  in  1  1/2  cups  tomato  juice,  break 
in  3  eggs,   add  half  cup  grated  onion,   1  tbs . 
chili  powder,  half  tsp.  nutmeg.     Season  to  taste 
with  celery  salt,   seasoning  salt,  and  accent. 
Add  1  tbs.  plain  salt  and  about  1  tsp.  black  pep- 
per.    Roll  walnut  sized  balls  of  meat  in  sifted 
bread  crumbs,   fry  about  six  minutes  in  hot  deep 
fat.     Serve  in  chafing  dish  with  heated  barbecue 
sauce.     Makes  about  200  to  250  meat  balls,  serves 
about  60  people.     Let  your  seasoning  suit  your 
own  personal  taste.     A  more  or  less  spicy  flavor 
can  be  achieved  by  varying  the  amount  of  spices. 
Always  cook  one  meat  ball  to  make  sure  it  tastes 
right  before  cooking  the  whole  batch.  They  can  be 
frozen  until  you  desire  to  use  them.
