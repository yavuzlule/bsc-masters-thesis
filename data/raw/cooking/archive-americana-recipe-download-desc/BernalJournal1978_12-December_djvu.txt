The Community Design Center's 
young and talented Bernal Heights 
team is winding up its six months of 
investigations and' surveys of the 
east slope. 

An amazing amount of data has 
been gathered, much of it from 
hundreds of private interviews and 
dozens of public meetings. Xhis data 
is now being collected into various 
issue-oriented work papers which are ' 
then distributed to interested 
community members. 

These task forces then select 
priorities and make suggestions on 
ways to deal with local concerns. 

A hill-wide open space proposal,, 
new legislation governing development 
on unimproved streets, a preliminary 
Community Development budget, and a 
trial system of neighborhood notifi- 
cation of new construction have already 
emerged from the CDC study. 

Suggestions for improved traffic 
circulation and contr.ol, better 
access and parking, pedestrian and 
public transportation recommendations, 
and even new street alignments are 
forthcoming. 

Guidelines concerning the 
rehabilitation of existing housing 
and the compatability of new con- 
struction are presently being 
discussed. 

Another task force will concen- 
trate on community safety programs 
and improved City services. 

While the CDC Study, was re- 
quested by the Mullen-Holladay Group . 
and then approved by the S.F. Planning 
Commission, it has 'grown beyond its 
original scope. 

Although the Mullen-Holladay 
Group represents a small section of 
the east slope, it is a section with 
many problems and continues to 
require a large amount of assistance 
from the CDC. 

The Design Center staff has 
encouraged the participation of a 
large number of the unorganized, 
non-meeting "attending, and usually 
silent residents. Owners and developers 
of the 250 vacant east slope lots are 
also being surveyed, along with a wide 



range of local leaders. 

While the Study itself is pro- 
ceeding according to schedule, guar- 
anteeing adequate time for the comman- 
ity to absorb all the information- arid 
agree upon an improvement plan may 
require more time then expected.

mation concerning the study or the 
opportunity to influence its recomen- 
dations is welcome to call the
