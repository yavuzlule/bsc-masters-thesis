Glen  Canyon  Park:  Meetings  About  Its  Future 

by  Barbara  Zierten-GPA  Recreation  &  Pork  Chair 


The  San  Francisco  Recreation  and  Park 
Department  held  two  community  meetings  at  the 
Glen  Park  Recreation  Center  to  discuss  a  process 
for  community  involvement  in  the  development  of 
a  Master  Plan  for  Glen  Canyon. 

The  first  meeting  was  held  on  November  9th. 
At  that  meeting,  Debra  Learner,  senior  planner 
with  Rec  and  Park,  introduced  Carolyn  Verheyen, 
a  facilitator  with  MIG,  Inc.,  a  Berkeley-based 
consulting  group  which  has  been  hired  by  the  City 
to  organize  community  involvement  regarding 
further  plans  for  Glen  Canyon.  Ms.  Verheyen 
presented  the  following  plan  structure.  A  working 
group  will  be  formed  consisting  of  representatives 
of  the  various  park  user  groups.  The  group's 
mission  will  be  to  meet  and  discuss  automobile 
access  and  protection  of  natural  resources  in  Glen 
Canyon.  It's  task  will  be  to  study  the  issues, 
discuss  alternative  solutions,  and  develop  a 
community-based  consensus  of  recommendations 
for  future  park  use. 

A  second  community  meeting  was  held  on 


December  6,  to  discuss,  confirm,  and  finalize 
which  groups  will  provide  representatives  to  the 
working  group.  The  participants  at  the  meeting 
agreed  that  the  following  park  user  groups  should 
be  represented  on  the  working  group:  the  Glen 
Park  Association,  Glen  Ridge  Nursery  School, 
Glen  Park  Rec  &  Park  staff,  Silvertree  Day  Camp, 
GP  DOG,  neighbors  living  adjacent  to  the  Park, 
Diamond  Heights  Community  Association, 
unaffiliated  park  users,  environmental  groups, 
unaffiliated  rec  center  users,  and  Neighbors  for  a 
Car  Free  Park. 

The  working  group  will  begin  meeting  in  early 
1996.  It  is  expected  that  the  group  will  meet  four 
or  five  times  to  discuss  the  issues  involved.  The 
Glen  Park  Association  will  be  selecting  a  delegate 
and  an  alternate  to  the  working  group  at  the 
January  9,  1996  GPA  meeting. 

Please  call  Barbara  Zierten  at  239-4235  if  you 
have  any  questions  or  comments  regarding  the 
process,  the  plan,  or  any  other  recreation  and  park 
issues.

NOVEMBER  SAFETY  MEETING 

Captain  Michael  Dower,  SFPD  Ingleside 
Station;  Captain  Bill  Welch,  oversees  all  major 
SFPD  felony  investigations  in  San  Francisco; 
and  Commander  Gary  Gee,  BART  police,  met 
with  more  than  150  Glen  Park  residents  at  the 
November  GPA  meeting.  The  Police 
Department  is  vigorously  investigating  Gary 
Pattock's  murder  in  October  1995,  and  is  still 
actively  pursuing  the  August  1994,  Joseph 
Jaworowicz  murder.  Fourteen  Police 
Inspectors  investigate  about  100  homicides 
annually,  and  they  solve  about  50  percent. 

Captain  Dower  announced  that  we  can 
expect  our  own  beat  officer  in  March  1996  so 
that  Glen  Park  will  have  a  regular  and 
continuing  police  presence.  He  also  reminded 
us  of  the  monthly  community  policing  meeting 
at  Ingleside  Police  Station,  and  he  encourages 
more  active  participation  from  the  Glen  Park 
neighborhood. 

The  representatives  from  SFPD  and  BART 
responded  to  many  questions  about  personal 
and  community  safety  but,  because  of  their 
current  status,  declined  to  provide  detailed 
responses  to  questions  about  the  two 
neighborhood  murders. 

Coming  in  March  1996: 

A  SFPD  beat  officer  for  a  regular  police 
presence  in  Glen  Park.

SEPTEMBER  CAMPFIRE

Alice  Gualco 

1921-1995 

Long  time  Glen  Park  resident  Alice  Gualco 
died  Decemher  22,  1995.  Alice  had  lived  on 
Paradise  Street,  across  from  Glen  Canyon 
Park,  for  35  years. 

Long  before  the  work  "activist"  came  into 
vogue,  Alice  concerned  herself  with 
neighborhood  affairs.  When  she  saw 
something  she  did  not  like  or  wanted  fixed, 
installed,  or  changed,  she  bird-dogged  the 
official  responsible  until  she  received 
satisfaction.  As  a  member  of  the  Glen  Park 
Property  Owners  Association  she  particularly 
concerned  herself  with  the  park,  helping  to 
prevent  the  widening  of  O'Shaughnessy  and  the 
destruction  of  a  great  deal  of  the  park  itself. 
She  continued  to  be  active  in  the  current  Glen 
Park  Association  until  recently. 

Alice  was  born  June  15,  1921  in  Meridan, 
Kansas.  She  served  as  a  storekeeper  in  the 
WACS  during  WWII.  Moving  to  San 
Francisco  in  the  early  195()'s  she  married  Frank 
and  lived  on  Arlington  Street  before  moving  to 
Paradise  Street  in  I960.  She  is  survived  by 
husband  Frank  and  sons  Frank  Jr.  and  Mike. 

Glen  Park  has  lost  a  loyal  and  committed 
resident,  neighbor  and  friend.

WINTER  RECIPE 

On  these  cold  winter  days,  there's  nothing 
better  than  a  cup  of  hot  tea  and  a  fresh  scone. 

Simple  Scones 

2  1/2  c.  all-purpose  flour 
1  Tbs.  baking  powder 
1/2  tsp.  salt 

1/2  c.  (one  stick)  cold,  unsalted  butter 

1/4  c.  sugar  (1/3  c.  if  you  prefer  them  sweeter ) 

2/3  c.  milk 

Preheat  oven  to  425°.  Mix  flour,  baking 
powder,  and  salt  in  a  large  bowl.  Slice  the 
butter  into  pieces  and,  using  fingers,  rub  into 
the  flour  (or  use  a  pastry  blender)  until  the 
mixture  resembles  small  gravel.  Add  the  sugar 
and  mix  well.  Add  the  milk  and  stir  using  a 
fork,  until  a  dough  forms. 

Take  dough  from  the  bowl  and  knead  15-20 
times  on  a  lightly  floured  surface.  Split  dough 
in  half,  form  balls  and  flatten  to  a  circle  of  6-8 
inch  diameter.  Cut  each  into  6  or  8  wedges  and 
arrange  on  an  ungreased  baking  sheet  with 
sides  slightly  touching.  Bake  10  -  12  minutes. 

Scones  should  appear  slightly  doughy  in  the 
middle  (they  will  continue  to  cook  once 
removed.  You  do  not  want  them  to  be  dry). 
Wrap  in  a  cloth  and  let  cool.  Serve  with  fresh 
whipped  cream,  butter  and  jam.  YUMMY!
